Golden Ages of the Tang and Song Dynasties

Compare and Contrast

Tang Dynasty (618–907)

  • Known for strong government and military power
  • Expanded China's territory
  • Capital city Chang'an was a major trade center
  • Golden age of poetry and art (Li Bai, Du Fu)
  • Silk Road trade increased cultural exchange
  • Buddhism became very influential

Similarities

  • Both were considered "Golden Ages" of prosperity
  • Advances in art, culture, and education
  • Strong central governments
  • Expanded trade and economic growth
  • Use of civil service exams
  • Improved technology and innovation

Song Dynasty (960–1279)

  • Focused more on economic growth than military strength
  • Major technological inventions (printing, compass, gunpowder)
  • Growth of cities and trade
  • Paper money introduced
  • Advances in farming increased food supply
  • Neo-Confucianism became important
